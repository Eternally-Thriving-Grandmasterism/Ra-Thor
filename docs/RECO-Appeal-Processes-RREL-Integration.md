## Executive Summary

After a **RECO Discipline Committee** decision or other sanction (administrative monetary penalty, registration condition, suspension, or revocation), the registrant has a statutory right to appeal.

**Primary Appeal Route (2026):**  
Most Discipline Committee decisions are appealed to the **Licence Appeal Tribunal (LAT)** within **30 days** of the decision. LAT can confirm, vary, or set aside the original order. Further appeals on questions of law go to the **Divisional Court**.

**RECO** can also appeal in limited circumstances.

### 2. Licence Appeal Tribunal (LAT) Hearing
- De novo or review hearing (depending on grounds).
- Evidence, witnesses, and legal submissions presented.
- LAT can confirm, vary, or set aside the Discipline Committee order.

- RREL’s immutable audit trail is presented as “proactive ethical governance” — often viewed very favorably by LAT adjudicators.

### 3. Divisional Court Appeal (Questions of Law Only)
- Further appeal from LAT decision on pure questions of law.

**RREL Integration:**
- Full Legal Lattice export with cryptographic timestamps provides irrefutable evidence of when and why every decision was made.
