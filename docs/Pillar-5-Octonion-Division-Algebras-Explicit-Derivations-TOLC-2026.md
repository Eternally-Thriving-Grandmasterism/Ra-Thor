## 1. Definition of Division Algebra

A (non-associative) algebra \(A\) over \(\mathbb{R}\) is a **division algebra** if for every non-zero \(x \in A\) there exists a (two-sided) inverse \(x^{-1}\) such that

\[
x \cdot x^{-1} = x^{-1} \cdot x = 1
\]

Equivalently, there are no zero divisors: \(x y = 0\) implies \(x = 0\) or \(y = 0\).

## 3. Proof of Two-Sided Inverse Property

Compute left inverse:

\[
x \cdot x^{-1} = x \cdot \left( \frac{\overline{x}}{|x|^2} \right) = \frac{1}{|x|^2} (x \overline{x}) = \frac{|x|^2}{|x|^2} = 1

Right inverse:
