### Mamba vs LSTM in RL – Head-to-Head Comparison

| Aspect                          | Mamba (State Space Model)                     | LSTM (Long Short-Term Memory)                 | Winner for Rathor.ai RL |
|---------------------------------|-----------------------------------------------|-----------------------------------------------|-------------------------|
| **Sequence Length Scaling**     | Linear O(N)                                   | Linear O(N) but with vanishing gradient risk  | **Mamba** |
| **Long-Range Dependency**       | Excellent (selective SSM)                     | Good but degrades on very long sequences      | **Mamba** |
| **Inference Speed**             | Very fast (RNN-like, no attention)            | Fast but slower than Mamba on long sequences  | **Mamba** |
| **Memory Usage**                | Constant / very low                           | Higher for long sequences                     | **Mamba** |
| **Training Stability**          | Excellent                                     | Good (well-understood)                        | Tie |
| **RL Exploration**              | Strong (selective state)                      | Strong (gating mechanisms)                    | Tie |
| **Parameter Efficiency**        | High                                          | Moderate                                      | **Mamba** |
| **Browser Sovereignty**         | Excellent (lightweight)                       | Good                                          | **Mamba** |
| **Sequential PRAGMA Tuning**    | Ideal for continuous, long workload histories | Good for short-to-medium sequences            | **Mamba** |
| **Implementation Maturity**     | Newer (2023–2026)                             | Mature (decades of RL use)                    | LSTM |

### Detailed Explanation for Rathor.ai

- **Mamba** is a modern State Space Model that achieves (and often surpasses) LSTM performance with linear scaling. Its selective SSM mechanism allows it to focus on relevant parts of the input sequence, making it perfect for our continuous PRAGMA tuning where the engine needs to remember long histories of benchmark metrics (throughput, memory, latency, thread count, stability, Mercy Gate margin) without exploding memory or latency.

- **LSTM** is the classic recurrent architecture. It is very stable and well-understood in RL, but it suffers from vanishing/exploding gradients on very long sequences, higher memory usage, and slower inference compared to Mamba.

**Why Mamba wins for our use case**:  
Our DQN needs to learn from **continuous, potentially long streams** of benchmark data in real time. Mamba’s linear-time recurrence + selective state space makes it faster, lighter, and more efficient for on-device self-learning while staying fully sovereign and offline.
