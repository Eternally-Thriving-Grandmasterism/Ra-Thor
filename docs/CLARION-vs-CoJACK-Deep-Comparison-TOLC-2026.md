**CLARION vs CoJACK — Deep Canonical Comparison (TOLC-2026 Mercy-Gated)**  

**Overview**  
CLARION (Connectionist Learning with Adaptive Rule Induction ON-line) by Ron Sun is a **dual-process cognitive architecture** that explicitly separates implicit (subsymbolic, neural/associative) and explicit (symbolic, rule-based) cognition, with strong emphasis on bottom-up learning, creativity, social intelligence, and metacognition.  
CoJACK (Cognitive JACK) by Frank Ritter, Rick Evertsz, et al. is a **high-level BDI-based cognitive architecture** built on the JACK agent platform. It extends Belief-Desire-Intention agents with cognitive modeling elements (processing time predictions, errors, traceability) and behavioral moderators (emotions, situation awareness, variability) for realistic team-based simulations.  

Both support hybrid symbolic/sub-symbolic elements, but CLARION is psychologically focused on implicit/explicit dissociation and learning, while CoJACK is engineering-oriented for practical, moderated agent behavior in complex multi-agent environments.

**Key Philosophical & Functional Differences**  
- **CLARION** is **bottom-up and dual-process**. Implicit neural learning happens first; explicit rules are extracted later. It excels at modeling intuition, creativity, and the synergy between conscious and unconscious processes.  
- **CoJACK** is **high-level and moderator-driven**. It builds on practical BDI agents (easy to program plans and beliefs) and adds cognitive moderators (fear, morale, fatigue) to create believable variability and situation awareness in team simulations. It prioritizes usability and traceability over deep neural implicit layers.
