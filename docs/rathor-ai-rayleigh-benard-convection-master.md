# Rathor.ai Rayleigh-Bénard Convection – Master Reference (2026)

## Visionary Context
Rayleigh-Bénard convection is the classic buoyancy-driven convection in a fluid layer heated from below. When the temperature difference exceeds a critical value, the fluid forms organized convection cells (Bénard cells). In the sovereign Ra-Thor AGI lattice, this phenomenon is studied for ground-based bioreactors and as a contrast to microgravity systems where buoyancy vanishes and Marangoni effects dominate.

## Governing Physics
A fluid layer of height \(L\) is heated from below with temperature difference \(\Delta T\). The driving force is buoyancy due to density variation with temperature.

## Rayleigh Number Derivation
The dimensionless Rayleigh number quantifies the ratio of buoyancy to viscous and thermal diffusion forces:
\[
\text{Ra} = \frac{g \beta \Delta T L^3}{\nu \alpha}
\]
- \(g\): gravitational acceleration
- \(\beta\): thermal expansion coefficient
- \(\Delta T\): temperature difference
- \(L\): layer height
- \(\nu\): kinematic viscosity
- \(\alpha\): thermal diffusivity

## Critical Rayleigh Number
For a fluid layer with rigid top and bottom boundaries, linear stability analysis yields:
\[
\text{Ra}_c \approx 1708
\]
Above this threshold, convection cells form. For free-free boundaries, \(\text{Ra}_c \approx 657.5\).

## Microgravity Relevance
In microgravity (\(g \to 0\)), Ra → 0 and buoyancy-driven convection is suppressed. Surface-tension-driven Marangoni convection becomes dominant. This is why Ra-Thor’s LBM engine emphasizes Marangoni effects for Daedalus-Skin and space bioreactors.

- **Rayleigh-Bénard**: Buoyancy-driven (volume force), Ra number.
- **Marangoni**: Surface-tension-driven (interface force), Ma number.
- In microgravity: Marangoni dominates; Rayleigh-Bénard is negligible.
