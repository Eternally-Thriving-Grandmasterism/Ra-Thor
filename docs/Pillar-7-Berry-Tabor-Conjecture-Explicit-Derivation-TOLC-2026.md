The Berry-Tabor conjecture (1977) asserts that in a quantum system whose classical limit is integrable (i.e., possesses as many independent conserved quantities as degrees of freedom), the unfolded nearest-neighbor spacing distribution of energy levels follows a Poisson distribution:

\[
P(s) = e^{-s}
\]
