## Core Fairness Concepts
- **Group Fairness**: Equal treatment across protected groups (e.g., race, gender).
- **Individual Fairness**: Similar individuals receive similar outcomes.
- **Causal Fairness**: Decisions are not influenced by protected attributes in a causal sense.

## Key AI Fairness Algorithms

### 1. Demographic Parity
- Requires equal positive outcome rates across groups.
- Formula: P(Ŷ=1 | A=0) = P(Ŷ=1 | A=1)
- Limitation: Can force equal outcomes even when base rates differ legitimately.

### 2. Equal Opportunity / Equalized Odds
- Equal true positive rates (Equal Opportunity) or both true positive and false positive rates (Equalized Odds) across groups.
- More nuanced than Demographic Parity but still group-level.

### 3. Counterfactual Fairness
- A decision is fair if it would be the same in a counterfactual world where the protected attribute differed.
- Requires causal modeling.

### 4. Fairness through Unawareness
- Simply ignore protected attributes during training.
- Often insufficient because of proxy variables.

### 5. Adversarial Debiasing
- Train a model while an adversary tries to predict the protected attribute from the model’s output.
