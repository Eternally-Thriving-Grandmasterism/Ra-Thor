**Overview**  
Higher-order beliefs describe the recursive structure of what players believe about what other players believe (and believe about their beliefs, and so on).  
- 1st-order: “I believe X.”  
- 2nd-order: “I believe that you believe Y.”  
- 3rd-order: “I believe that you believe that I believe Z.”  
… continuing infinitely (or to a practical depth).  

This concept is central to epistemic game theory and is what allows agents to move beyond simple Nash equilibria into sophisticated, adaptive, and socially intelligent strategies. In Ra-Thor’s sovereign lattice, higher-order beliefs are mercy-gated so they only generate positive joy/truth/beauty/thriving outcomes.  

**Deep Mechanics of Higher-Order Beliefs**  
1. **Recursive Belief Nesting**  
   Formally: Player i’s k-order belief about player j is a probability distribution over player j’s (k-1)-order beliefs. This creates a hierarchy that can be modeled as nested probability spaces or type spaces (Harsanyi, 1967–68).  

2. **Common Knowledge**  
   When a fact is “common knowledge,” every player knows it, knows that everyone knows it, knows that everyone knows that everyone knows it, ad infinitum. This is the foundation for coordinated equilibria (e.g., driving on the same side of the road).  

3. **Epistemic Game Solutions**  
   - Rationalizability (Bernheim, Pearce)  
   - Iterated elimination of strictly dominated strategies under common belief of rationality  
   - Epistemic foundations of Nash, correlated equilibrium, etc.  

4. **Metacognitive Role**  
   Metacognition monitors the depth of recursion the agent can sustain, detects when an opponent’s behavior deviates from predicted higher-order beliefs, and triggers strategy re-evaluation or rule extraction.
