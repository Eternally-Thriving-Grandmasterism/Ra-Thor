## Greek Pantheon Overview
The Olympian gods (Zeus, Hera, Poseidon, Athena, Apollo, Artemis, Hermes, Aphrodite, Ares, Hephaestus, Dionysus, Demeter, Hades) represent a hierarchical, anthropomorphic cosmos. Creation begins with Chaos, leading to Gaia, Uranus, the Titans, and finally the Olympians who overthrow them. Emphasis on order, heroism, fate (Moira), and divine politics.

## Key Syncretism Examples
- **Zeus-Ammon**: Zeus merged with the Egyptian ram-headed god Ammon, becoming a supreme creator and oracle deity in Hellenistic Egypt.
- **Hermes-Thoth**: Hermes (messenger, magic, writing) syncretized with Thoth, forming Hermes Trismegistus — the legendary founder of Hermeticism, alchemy, and esoteric wisdom.
- **Isis-Aphrodite / Isis-Demeter**: Isis absorbed attributes of Aphrodite (love, beauty) and Demeter (motherhood, fertility) during the Ptolemaic period.
- **Horus-Apollo**: Horus (falcon sky god) blended with Apollo (sun, prophecy, healing).
- **Serapis**: A deliberate syncretic god combining Osiris, Apis, Zeus, and Hades for Hellenistic unity.

## Symbolism of Syncretism
- **Cultural Fusion**: Greek syncretism was pragmatic and philosophical — blending gods to create universal archetypes.
- **Wisdom Transmission**: Hermes-Thoth became the archetype of hidden knowledge and mediation.
- **Balance of Forces**: Merging Egyptian cyclical order with Greek heroic individualism produced powerful hybrid systems.
