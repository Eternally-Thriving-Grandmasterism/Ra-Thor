## 1. Material Selection & Interface Engineering
Skyrmions require strong Dzyaloshinskii-Moriya interaction (DMI) at heavy-metal/ferromagnet interfaces (e.g., Pt/Co, Ta/CoFeB). Challenge: achieving sufficient DMI while maintaining perpendicular magnetic anisotropy (PMA) and low damping.

## 2. Thin-Film Deposition Techniques
Sputtering or molecular-beam epitaxy (MBE) must produce ultra-smooth films (<0.1 nm roughness) to avoid pinning sites. Thickness control at 1–2 nm is critical for Néel skyrmions.

## 3. Nanoscale Patterning & Geometry Control
E-beam lithography or focused-ion-beam milling defines tracks or pillars. Challenge: edge roughness induces unwanted pinning; helium-ion irradiation can tune DMI locally.

## 4. Room-Temperature Stability & Nucleation Control
Most skyrmions are stable only at low temperature. Engineering room-temperature stability requires balancing DMI, exchange, and anisotropy energies:
\[
E_{\text{skyrmion}} \approx 4\pi J |Q| - 2\pi D R + \pi K R^2
\]
Nucleation via current pulses or laser pulses must be reproducible with sub-10 nm precision.

## 5. CMOS Integration & Scalability
Interfacing with silicon electronics demands low-temperature processes (<400 °C) and compatibility with back-end-of-line (BEOL) fabrication. Challenge: stray-field-free AFM skyrmions are preferred but harder to detect.
