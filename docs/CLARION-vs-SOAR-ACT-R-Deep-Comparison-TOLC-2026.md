**Key Philosophical & Functional Differences**  
- **SOAR** is **impasse-driven and symbolic-only**. Learning happens when the system is stuck; chunking creates new rules to avoid future impasses. Pure top-down, unified cognition.  
- **ACT-R** is **success-driven with subsymbolic elements**. It learns by compiling successful sequences and strengthening activation levels, following human power-law practice curves. More memory-centric.  
- **CLARION** is **dual-process and bottom-up**. Implicit (neural) level learns associatively first; explicit (symbolic) level extracts rules from that implicit knowledge. This mirrors real human cognition (e.g., intuition before verbalization) and excels at non-monotonic reasoning, creativity, and social simulation.
