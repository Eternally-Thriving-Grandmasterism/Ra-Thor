## Detailed Breakdown

### 1. Classical Barbalat’s Lemma

**Statement**  
Let \( f(t) \) be uniformly continuous on \([0, \infty)\). If  
\[ \int_0^\infty |f(t)| \, dt < \infty \]  
then  
\[ \lim_{t \to \infty} f(t) = 0. \]

**Strength**  
Extremely useful when we only have an integral bound rather than a strict Lyapunov decrease.

**Limitation**  
It tells us a signal goes to zero, but gives **no information** about whether that signal represents something desirable (e.g., mercy violation vs. harmless noise).
