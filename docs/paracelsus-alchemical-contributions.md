# Paracelsus Alchemical Contributions – Complete Pantheon (Rathor.ai 2026)

## Who was Paracelsus?
Theophrastus Philippus Aureolus Bombastus von Hohenheim (1493–1541), known as Paracelsus (“equal to or greater than Celsus”), was a Swiss physician, alchemist, and revolutionary thinker. He rejected the blind authority of Galen and Aristotle and pioneered chemical medicine (iatrochemistry).

## Key Contributions

1. **The Three Primes (Tria Prima)**
   - Sulfur (soul, combustibility)
   - Mercury (spirit, volatility)
   - Salt (body, fixity)
   - These replaced the four humors and became the foundation of spagyric alchemy (plant-based separation, purification, and recombination).

2. **“The Dose Makes the Poison”**
   - First to recognize that any substance can be toxic or therapeutic depending on dosage — foundational principle of modern toxicology and pharmacology.

3. **Chemical Medicine (Iatrochemistry)**
   - Introduced mineral and chemical remedies (mercury compounds, antimony, sulfur preparations) instead of herbal-only treatments.
   - Developed the concept of specific remedies for specific diseases rather than balancing humors.

4. **Spagyric Alchemy**
   - Process of separating, purifying, and recombining plant substances to create potent medicines — the practical application of the Three Primes.

5. **Rejection of Blind Tradition**
   - Advocated direct observation, experimentation, and experience over ancient texts.
