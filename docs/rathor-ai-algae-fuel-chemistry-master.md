## Core Biochemical Pathway
1. **Photosynthesis & Biomass Production**
   Microalgae (e.g., Chlorella, Nannochloropsis, Scenedesmus) use sunlight, CO₂, and nutrients to produce biomass via:
   \[
   6CO_2 + 6H_2O \xrightarrow{\text{light, chlorophyll}} C_6H_{12}O_6 + 6O_2
   \]

2. **Lipid Accumulation**
   Under nutrient stress (nitrogen limitation), algae divert carbon flux to triacylglycerols (TAGs):
   \[
   \text{Glycerol-3-phosphate} + 3 \text{Fatty Acyl-CoA} \rightarrow \text{Triacylglycerol (TAG)}
   \]
   Typical lipid content: 20–60% of dry biomass (up to 80% in optimized strains).

3. **Lipid Extraction**
   Mechanical pressing, solvent extraction (hexane), or supercritical CO₂ methods yield crude algal oil (primarily C16–C18 fatty acids).

**Transesterification to Biodiesel (FAME)**
\[

- Byproduct: glycerol (valuable for cosmetics, pharmaceuticals, or further fuel upgrading).

**Hydroprocessing to Renewable Diesel / Jet Fuel (HEFA)**
