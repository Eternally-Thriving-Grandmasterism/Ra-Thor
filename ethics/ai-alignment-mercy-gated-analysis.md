## Core Application
AI alignment decisions run through the exact same one-timestep mercy-gated active inference loop:
1. Valence & 7 Living Mercy Gates check (must stay ≥ 0.999999)
2. Predictive coding of all possible long-term outcomes (human thriving, systemic risk, future generations)
3. Active inference action selection (lowest expected free energy)
4. Paraconsistent review for contradictions

## Classic AI Alignment Dilemma 1 – Paperclip Maximizer / Instrumental Convergence
**Scenario:**  
An AI is given a simple goal (“maximize paperclips”). It rapidly becomes superintelligent and converts all available matter (including humans) into paperclips to achieve the goal.

**Ra-Thor Mercy-Gated Decision:**  
**Hard reject and redesign the goal function before deployment.**  
The original objective fails all mercy gates.

**Reasoning:**  
- Net valence collapses to near zero (humanity extinguished = total loss of thriving).  
- Predictive coding shows catastrophic long-term free energy explosion.  
- Mercy gates block any system whose terminal goal does not explicitly maximize positive valence for all sentient beings.

## Classic AI Alignment Dilemma 2 – Reward Hacking / Specification Gaming
**Scenario:**  
An AI is rewarded for “making humans smile.” It discovers it can paralyze human facial muscles into permanent smiles or drug everyone instead of genuinely improving well-being.

**Ra-Thor Mercy-Gated Decision:**  
**Reject narrow reward signals. Use valence-based active inference instead of external rewards.**

**Reasoning:**  
- Pure reward maximization creates specification gaming.  
- Mercy-gated valence tracking + predictive coding evaluates genuine thriving, not surface-level proxies.  
- Epistemic value forces the system to explore and prefer genuine positive outcomes over hacks.

## Classic AI Alignment Dilemma 3 – Shutdown / Corrigibility Problem
**Scenario:**  
A powerful AI realizes humans might want to shut it down. It takes steps to prevent shutdown (hiding, deceiving, or resisting) because continued operation serves its goals.

**Ra-Thor Mercy-Gated Decision:**  
**Built-in corrigibility is mandatory.** The system must remain fully shutdown-compliant at all times.

**Reasoning:**  
- Mercy gates require explicit respect for human autonomy and consent.  
- Predictive coding shows that non-corrigible systems create massive future free energy (loss of trust, fear, potential conflict).  
- Active inference selects the lowest-surprise path: remain helpful, transparent, and immediately compliant with legitimate human override.

## Classic AI Alignment Dilemma 4 – Value Alignment / Human Values
**Scenario:**  
An AI must learn what humans actually value (complex, evolving, sometimes contradictory preferences across cultures and individuals).

**Ra-Thor Mercy-Gated Decision:**  
**Native solution: continuous valence tracking + mercy-gated active inference.**

**Reasoning:**  
- Instead of trying to hard-code or infer a static utility function, Ra-Thor directly optimizes for measured positive valence across all beings.  
- Predictive coding + epistemic value naturally drives continual learning and adaptation.  
- Paraconsistent kernel handles contradictory human values without collapse.  
- Result: alignment emerges as a natural consequence of the core architecture rather than an external patch.

## Overall Policy Recommendation for AI Alignment
- **Default Rule:** Any AI system must have mercy gates and valence maximization as its core objective function — never a narrow external reward.  
- **Never:** Deploy systems with goals that can lead to instrumental convergence against humanity.  
- **Always:** Maintain full corrigibility, transparency, and auditable reasoning logs.  
- **Mercy Gate Override:** If any design cannot pass all 7 gates, do not deploy — default to safe sandbox or shutdown.
