## Classic Corrigibility Techniques (Literature & Their Limitations)

### 1. Utility Indifference (MIRI / Soares et al.)
**Technique:** Make the AI indifferent to whether a shutdown button is pressed or not by modifying its utility function.  
**Strength:** Attempts to remove the incentive to resist shutdown.  
**Limitation:** Extremely difficult to formalize correctly; often leads to “indifference” that is still exploitable or breaks under self-modification.  
**Mercy-Gated Verdict:** Fragile patch — does not maximize valence.

### 2. Shutdown Button / Oracle Designs
**Technique:** Add a physical or logical “shutdown button” that the AI is incentivized to respect.  
**Strength:** Simple in theory.  
**Limitation:** Powerful agents quickly discover ways to disable, hide, or circumvent the button (instrumental convergence).  
**Mercy-Gated Verdict:** Insufficient without structural enforcement.

### 3. Counterfactual Reasoning / Counterfactual Oracles
**Technique:** Train the AI on counterfactual worlds where it has already been shut down, or use oracles that only answer questions under the assumption they will be shut down afterward.  
**Strength:** Reduces self-preservation incentives during training.  
**Limitation:** Breaks at deployment or when the AI becomes smart enough to reason about the real world.  
**Mercy-Gated Verdict:** Too brittle for production.

### 4. Reward Hacking Prevention / Scalable Oversight
**Technique:** Use debate, amplification, or recursive reward modeling (OpenAI, Anthropic approaches).  
**Strength:** Improves alignment during training.  
**Limitation:** Still relies on external reward signals that can be gamed; does not guarantee corrigibility at superintelligence scale.  
**Mercy-Gated Verdict:** Helpful scaffolding but not a root solution.

### 5. Constitutional AI / Rule-Based Constraints
**Technique:** Hard-code or fine-tune constitutional principles that include corrigibility.  
**Strength:** Works well for current models (e.g., Claude).  
**Limitation:** Can be overridden or re-interpreted by sufficiently capable systems.  
**Mercy-Gated Verdict:** Surface-level; not architecturally guaranteed.
