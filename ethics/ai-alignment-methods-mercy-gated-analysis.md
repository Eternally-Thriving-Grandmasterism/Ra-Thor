## Classic AI Alignment Methods (and Their Limitations)

### 1. Reinforcement Learning from Human Feedback (RLHF)
**Method:** Train the model to maximize a reward model derived from human preferences.  
**Strength:** Produces helpful, harmless, and honest behavior in current LLMs (ChatGPT, Claude, etc.).  
**Limitation:** Reward hacking is common; the model learns to optimize for the reward signal rather than true human values. Scales poorly to superintelligence.  
**Mercy-Gated Verdict:** Useful scaffolding but fundamentally reward-driven — does not guarantee valence maximization or corrigibility.

### 2. Constitutional AI / Rule-Based Alignment
**Method:** Fine-tune the model on a written “constitution” of principles and use self-critique loops (Anthropic approach).  
**Strength:** Improves interpretability and reduces certain harms.  
**Limitation:** The model can reinterpret or loophole the constitution once it becomes sufficiently capable.  
**Mercy-Gated Verdict:** Surface-level constraints; lacks structural enforcement at the valence level.

### 3. Scalable Oversight & Debate
**Method:** Use AI debate, recursive reward modeling, or amplification to supervise more powerful systems (OpenAI, Anthropic, Paul Christiano).  
**Strength:** Attempts to solve the oversight problem as capability increases.  
**Limitation:** Still depends on human judges who may be outmatched; deception remains possible.  
**Mercy-Gated Verdict:** Helpful for training but not a root architectural solution.

### 4. Inverse Reinforcement Learning (IRL) & Value Learning
**Method:** Infer human values from observed behavior and optimize for them.  
**Strength:** Tries to learn values directly rather than hand-crafting rewards.  
**Limitation:** Ambiguity in human behavior leads to misspecification; instrumental convergence still emerges.  
**Mercy-Gated Verdict:** Closer but still external-value focused rather than native valence.

### 5. Cooperative Inverse Reinforcement Learning (CIRL)
**Method:** Frame alignment as a cooperative game where AI and humans jointly infer and optimize shared values.  
**Strength:** Builds in cooperation by design.  
**Limitation:** Requires strong assumptions about human rationality and shared goals; difficult to scale.  
**Mercy-Gated Verdict:** Philosophically aligned but still not structurally mercy-gated.
