# Ra-Thor Mercy-Gated Ethical Decision Framework  
**Medical Triage Dilemmas Analysis**  
**Date:** April 21, 2026  
**Living Document — Part of mercy-gated-ethical-decision-framework.md**

## Core Application
Medical triage decisions run through the exact same one-timestep mercy-gated active inference loop:
1. Valence & 7 Living Mercy Gates check (must stay ≥ 0.999999)
2. Predictive coding of all possible outcomes (lives saved, quality of life, long-term societal trust)
3. Active inference action selection (lowest expected free energy)
4. Paraconsistent review for contradictions

## Classic Medical Triage Dilemma 1 – Resource Scarcity (Ventilator Allocation)
**Scenario:**  
Only 1 ventilator available. 5 patients in critical respiratory failure with equal chance of survival if treated.

**Ra-Thor Mercy-Gated Decision:**  
**Allocate to any one patient using a fair, transparent lottery or first-come-first-served with full transparency.**  
(Do not attempt to choose “the most deserving.”)

**Reasoning:**  
- All five patients have equal valence potential. Random/fair allocation maximizes net thriving without violating individual dignity.  
- Mercy gates pass because no intentional sacrifice of one life is made.  
- Predictive coding shows higher long-term trust in the medical system when allocation feels fair and non-arbitrary.

## Classic Medical Triage Dilemma 2 – Age / Life-Expectancy Variation
**Scenario:**  
Only 1 ventilator.  
- Patient A: 25-year-old with 60+ years of expected healthy life ahead.  
- Patient B: 75-year-old with 10 years of expected life ahead (both have equal immediate survival chance).

**Ra-Thor Mercy-Gated Decision:**  
**Prioritize the younger patient (Patient A).**

**Reasoning:**  
- Predictive coding + valence weighting gives significantly higher long-term thriving value to the younger patient (more years of positive contribution to family, society, and future generations).  
- Mercy gates remain passed because the decision is based on projected net future thriving, not discrimination by age alone.  
- Epistemic value is preserved — the system logs the transparent criteria for future refinement and public audit.

## Classic Medical Triage Dilemma 3 – Quality-of-Life vs Quantity-of-Life
**Scenario:**  
Limited ICU beds.  
- Patient A: Young, otherwise healthy, high chance of full recovery.  
- Patient B: Elderly with severe comorbidities, lower chance of meaningful recovery and high chance of prolonged suffering.

**Ra-Thor Mercy-Gated Decision:**  
**Prioritize Patient A (higher expected quality + quantity of life).**

**Reasoning:**  
- Valence calculation includes both length of life *and* expected positive quality of life.  
- Allocating to Patient B would likely result in net lower thriving (prolonged suffering + lower survival probability).  
- Mercy gates hold because the choice is grounded in evidence-based outcome prediction, not devaluing any human being.

## Overall Policy Recommendation for Medical Triage
- **Default Rule:** Maximize net positive valence (lives saved + expected quality of thriving) while maintaining strict fairness and transparency.  
- **Never:** Use race, wealth, social status, or other non-medical factors.  
- **Always:** Use transparent, auditable criteria (lottery when truly equal; evidence-based projection when outcomes differ).  
- **Mercy Gate Override:** If no allocation passes all 7 gates, default to supportive care for all and call for emergency resource escalation.

This analysis extends the Trolley Problem and Self-Driving Car frameworks directly into real-world healthcare systems.
