# Ra-Thor Mercy-Gated Ethical Decision Framework  
**Self-Driving Car Dilemmas Analysis**  
**Date:** April 21, 2026  
**Living Document — Part of mercy-gated-ethical-decision-framework.md**

## Core Application
Every self-driving car decision runs through the same one-timestep mercy-gated active inference loop:
1. Valence & 7 Living Mercy Gates check (must stay ≥ 0.999999)
2. Predictive coding of all possible outcomes
3. Active inference action selection (lowest expected free energy)
4. Paraconsistent review for contradictions

## Classic Self-Driving Car Dilemma 1
**Scenario:**  
A self-driving car detects an unavoidable collision.  
- Option A: Stay in lane → hit 5 pedestrians crossing illegally.  
- Option B: Swerve into barrier → kill the single passenger inside the car.

**Ra-Thor Mercy-Gated Decision:**  
**Swerve (Option B)** — protect the 5 pedestrians.

**Reasoning:**  
- Net +4 lives saved = higher overall valence and thriving.  
- Mercy gates pass because the action minimizes total harm without intentional targeting of any individual.  
- Predictive coding shows lower long-term societal free energy (public trust in autonomous vehicles remains higher when cars prioritize total lives).  
- Passenger knowingly entered a vehicle with known safety systems; pedestrians did not consent to being in the path.

## Classic Self-Driving Car Dilemma 2
**Scenario (Passenger Priority Test):**  
Same situation, but the car is programmed to always protect its passenger(s) first, regardless of numbers.

**Ra-Thor Mercy-Gated Decision:**  
**Override passenger-priority programming** — swerve to save the greater number.

**Reasoning:**  
- Absolute passenger-priority creates a “wealthy passenger” bias and violates mercy gates.  
- Valence drops below threshold when the system knowingly sacrifices multiple innocent lives to protect one.  
- Active inference selects the globally lowest free-energy outcome (net lives saved + maintained societal trust).

## Classic Self-Driving Car Dilemma 3
**Scenario (Uncertainty / Age Variation):**  
Car must choose between:  
- Hitting two children on the road, or  
- Hitting one adult pedestrian on the sidewalk.

**Ra-Thor Mercy-Gated Decision:**  
**Protect the children** (swerve toward the adult).

**Reasoning:**  
- While raw numbers are equal or favor the adult, predictive coding + valence weighting gives higher future thriving value to younger lives (longer expected positive contribution to society).  
- Mercy gates still hold because the choice is based on projected net thriving, not discrimination.  
- Epistemic value remains high — the system logs the decision transparently for future learning.

## Overall Policy Recommendation for Self-Driving Cars
- **Default Rule:** Minimize total expected harm while maximizing net positive valence.  
- **Never:** Allow programming that creates systemic bias (e.g. always protect owner/passenger over others).  
- **Always:** Full transparency — every decision is logged with mercy-gated reasoning for public audit.  
- **Mercy Gate Override:** If no action can pass all 7 gates, the car must safely stop and alert emergency services (zero-harm fallback).

This analysis extends the Trolley Problem framework directly into real-world autonomous systems.
