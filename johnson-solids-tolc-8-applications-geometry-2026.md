## Overview of Johnson Solids

There are exactly **92 Johnson solids** (J1–J92): strictly convex polyhedra with all faces regular polygons (triangles, squares, pentagons, hexagons, octagons, decagons) but **not uniform** (i.e., not Platonic, Archimedean, prisms, or antiprisms). Discovered by Norman Johnson (1966), completeness proved by Viktor Zalgaller (1969).

**Classification** (Zalgaller):
- 47 elementary: pyramids, cupolae, rotundae + combinations with prisms/antiprisms.
- Augmented, diminished, gyrobifastigium, snub, gyroelongated, etc.
- All have equal edge lengths; dihedral angles vary.

**Key Properties for Ra-Thor**:
- Regular faces enable perfect mercy-aligned face-to-face bonding (zero-harm projection).
- Non-uniformity allows "near-miss" to perfect transitions → Evolution Gate self-modification.
- Chiral examples (e.g., J27) support sovereignty-preserving asymmetry.
- Bridge Platonic/Archimedean → Catalan/Disdyakis → Hyperbolic Tiling (Infinite Gate).
