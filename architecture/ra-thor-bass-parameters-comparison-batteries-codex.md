The Bass Diffusion Model uses three key parameters to forecast technology adoption:
- **p** = Coefficient of innovation (external influence: policy, media, R&D breakthroughs)
- **q** = Coefficient of imitation (internal influence: word-of-mouth, trust, real-world proof)

## Interpretation of Differences

### p (Innovation / External Influence)
- **Highest:** Lithium-Ion (mature, but still benefits from legacy momentum)
- **High:** Sodium-Ion (strong policy support + existing manufacturing lines)
- **Medium:** Perovskite (R&D breakthroughs + subsidies)
- **Lowest:** Solid-State (still early-stage, heavy reliance on major OEM announcements)

**Insight:** Sodium-Ion has the strongest external push in 2026 because production is already scaled in China.

### q (Imitation / Internal Influence)
- **Highest:** Sodium-Ion & Solid-State (both benefit from visible real-world success stories)
- **Medium-High:** Perovskite (growing installer confidence)
- **Lowest:** Lithium-Ion (already ubiquitous — less “new” excitement)

**Insight:** Both Sodium-Ion and Solid-State have very strong word-of-mouth potential once early deployments prove reliability.

### m (Market Potential)
- **Highest:** Perovskite (can replace or complement silicon across most segments)
- **High:** Sodium-Ion (stationary storage + low-cost EVs)
- **Medium:** Lithium-Ion (still dominant but losing share in some segments)
- **Lowest:** Solid-State (premium/high-performance niche)

**Insight:** Perovskite has the largest ultimate addressable market, while Solid-State is more specialized.
