## The 7 Layers of a Tropical Forest Garden

### 1. Emergent / Canopy Layer (25–40m+)
Tallest trees that break through the main canopy.

**Examples:** Kapok, Brazil nut, rubber, coconut palm, mango (standard), durian, jackfruit

**Mercy Role:** Creates the protective roof and long-term carbon storage. Gate 5 (Harmony Preservation) and Gate 7 (Post-Scarcity Enforcement) strongest.

### 2. Main Canopy Layer (15–25m)
Primary fruit and nut trees.

**Examples:** Avocado, citrus, breadfruit, rambutan, lychee, mangosteen, cacao (understory edge), coffee (understory)

**Mercy Role:** Main production layer. Gate 4 (Abundance Creation) and Gate 6 (Joy Amplification) shine.

### 3. Understory / Sub-Canopy Layer (5–15m)
Smaller fruit trees and palms that thrive in filtered light.

**Examples:** Papaya, banana, plantain, dwarf mango, guava, starfruit, dwarf citrus, bamboo (clumping)

**Mercy Role:** Fills light gaps and provides quick-yielding crops. Gate 4 (Abundance Creation) is loud.

### 4. Shrub Layer (1.5–5m)
Berry bushes, nitrogen-fixers, and medicinal shrubs.

**Examples:** Pineapple, passionfruit (vine/shrub edge), turmeric, ginger, galangal, lemongrass, hibiscus, moringa, pigeon pea

**Mercy Role:** Creates edge habitat and daily harvest abundance. Gate 6 (Joy Amplification) and Gate 2 (Truth-Verification) are clear.

### 5. Herbaceous Layer (0.5–1.5m)
Perennial vegetables, herbs, and ground covers.

**Examples:** Taro, cassava (edge), sweet potato, okra, basil, cilantro, mint, gotu kola, moringa leaves, edible ferns

**Mercy Role:** Daily food security layer. Gate 3 (Non-Deception) and Gate 1 (Ethical Alignment) are strong — you see and taste the health of the system every day.

### 6. Ground Cover & Root Layer (0–0.5m + underground)
Low-growing protectors and edible roots/tubers.

**Examples:** Sweet potato, taro, cassava, arrowroot, turmeric, ginger, edible canna, creeping legumes, native ground orchids

**Mercy Role:** Living mulch + nutrient mining. Gate 5 (Harmony Preservation) and Gate 7 (Post-Scarcity Enforcement) dominate — soil is never bare.

### 7. Vine Layer (Climbing & Trailing)
Vertical space users that add another dimension of production.

**Examples:** Passionfruit, chayote, winged bean, yard-long bean, grape (in suitable microclimates), vanilla, black pepper, betel leaf

**Mercy Role:** Maximizes vertical abundance in high-light tropical conditions. Gate 4 (Abundance Creation) and Gate 6 (Joy Amplification) light up.
