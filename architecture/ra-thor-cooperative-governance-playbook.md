## Phase 1: Foundation & Legal Formation (4–8 weeks)

### Step 1: Define Your Cooperative Vision & Values
- Hold 2–3 community visioning sessions
- Answer: What does “thriving for all beings” mean in our context?
- Document core values (use the TOLC 7 Gates as a starting framework)

### Step 2: Choose Legal Structure
**Recommended Starting Point (2026):**
- **Energy Cooperative** (one-member, one-vote) — highest mercy alignment
- Or **Multi-Stakeholder Cooperative** if you have multiple key groups (residents + local businesses + municipality)

**Legal forms to consider:**
- Cooperative corporation
- LLC taxed as cooperative
- Community Benefit Corporation (with cooperative bylaws)

### Step 3: Draft Founding Documents
- Articles of Incorporation / Organization
- Bylaws (include mercy-gated decision thresholds)
- Membership agreement
- Initial capital contribution rules

**Ra-Thor Tool:** Ask Ra-Thor to generate draft bylaws using the latest Cooperative Ownership Models Codex.

## Phase 2: Governance Structure Design (3–6 weeks)

### Core Governance Bodies
1. **General Assembly** (all members) — highest authority, meets quarterly
2. **Board of Directors** (5–9 elected members) — strategic oversight
3. **Technical & Operations Committee** — day-to-day management
4. **Ethics & Mercy Committee** (new in 2026) — ensures all decisions pass through Mercy Gates

### Decision-Making Framework (Recommended)
- **Routine decisions:** Board + Operations Committee (simple majority)
- **Major decisions** (capital >$50k, new technology, bylaws changes): General Assembly (2/3 majority)
- **Mercy-Gated decisions** (anything affecting long-term thriving): Requires explicit mercy valence calculation via Ra-Thor tools

## Phase 3: Financial Governance & Profit Distribution (2–4 weeks)

### Capital Structure
- Member shares (affordable entry, limited resale value)
- Community bonds
- Retained earnings for reinvestment

### Profit Distribution (Mercy-Gated)
1. 40–50% reinvested into system improvement & expansion
2. 25–35% returned as patronage refunds to members
3. 15–20% allocated to community benefit fund (local projects, education, low-income support)
4. 5–10% reserved for emergency / resilience fund
