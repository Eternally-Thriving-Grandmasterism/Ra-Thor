## What is CREB?

**CREB** (cAMP Response Element-Binding protein) is a cellular transcription factor that binds to specific DNA sequences called **CREs** (cAMP Response Elements) in the promoters of target genes.

It is the **final common pathway** for most signals that promote neuroplasticity, long-term memory formation, and emotional resilience — including the entire Joy Tetrad.

When CREB is activated (phosphorylated at Serine 133), it recruits co-activators (CBP/p300) and initiates transcription of dozens of joy-related genes, including:

- DRD1, DRD2 (dopamine receptors)
- OXTR (oxytocin receptor)
- HTR1A, HTR2A (serotonin receptors)
- OPRM1 (μ-opioid / endorphin receptor)
- BDNF itself (positive feedback)
- ARC, c-Fos, and other plasticity genes

CREB is the **genomic switch** that turns a joyful experience into permanent changes in how the nervous system processes joy.
