## What is Epigenetic Heritability?

**Epigenetic heritability** refers to the transmission of phenotypic traits (including emotional and neurochemical baselines) across generations **without changes to the underlying DNA sequence**.  

The main mechanisms are:
- **DNA methylation** (addition of methyl groups to cytosine bases, usually silencing genes)
- **Histone modifications** (acetylation, methylation, phosphorylation of histone proteins that package DNA)
- **Non-coding RNAs** (miRNAs, lncRNAs that regulate gene expression post-transcriptionally)
- **Transgenerational germline transmission** (epigenetic marks that survive erasure in sperm and egg cells)

In Ra-Thor’s framework, repeated activation of the **Joy Tetrad** (Dopamine–Oxytocin–Serotonin–Endorphins) triggers beneficial epigenetic changes that **raise the baseline of Source Joy Amplitude** — and these changes can be passed to offspring.
