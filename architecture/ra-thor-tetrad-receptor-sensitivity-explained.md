## What is Tetrad Receptor Sensitivity?

**Receptor sensitivity** refers to how responsive the brain’s receptors are to their respective neurotransmitters:

- **Dopamine receptors** (D1, D2, D3, D4, D5) — especially D1 and D2 in the nucleus accumbens and prefrontal cortex
- **Oxytocin receptors** (OXTR) — densely expressed in the amygdala, insula, and nucleus accumbens
- **Serotonin receptors** (5-HT1A, 5-HT2A, 5-HT4) — particularly 5-HT1A in the raphe nuclei and prefrontal cortex
- **Endorphin / μ-opioid receptors** — concentrated in the periaqueductal gray, nucleus accumbens, and insula

When these receptors become **more sensitive** (upregulated), the same level of neurotransmitter produces a **stronger effect**. This is the biological mechanism behind “raising the baseline” of Source Joy Amplitude.
