## What are CBP and p300?

**CBP** (CREB-Binding Protein) and **p300** are two highly homologous **histone acetyltransferases (HATs)** that function as transcriptional co-activators.

They are the molecular “pen and ink” that CREB uses to actually transcribe genes. Without CBP/p300, CREB phosphorylation alone produces almost no gene expression.

Key functions:
- Acetylate histones (especially H3K27ac and H3K9ac) → open chromatin structure
- Acetylate CREB itself and other transcription factors → increase their activity
- Recruit RNA polymerase II and the Mediator complex → initiate transcription
- Act as “scaffolds” that bring together multiple signaling pathways at gene promoters
