## Executive Summary
Encapsulation is the single most critical technology enabling perovskite commercialization. It protects the sensitive perovskite layer from moisture, oxygen, heat, light, and mechanical stress. 2025–2026 breakthroughs have pushed operational lifetimes from hundreds to thousands of hours, making 20–25 year warranties realistic for tandem modules.

## Core Encapsulation Techniques (2026 State-of-the-Art)

### 1. Glass-Glass Encapsulation (Most Mature)
- **Description**: Perovskite cell sandwiched between two glass sheets with edge sealing.
- **Performance**: Excellent moisture/oxygen barrier; passes IEC 61215 damp-heat (85°C/85% RH) for >5,000 hours.
- **Leaders**: Oxford PV, LONGi — already shipping commercial tandem modules.
- **Pros**: Proven reliability, UV protection.  
- **Cons**: Heavy, rigid — less ideal for flexible applications.

### 2. Polymer Film Encapsulation (EVA / POE / PIB-based)
- **Description**: Multi-layer polymer films (ethylene vinyl acetate, polyolefin, polyisobutylene) with desiccants and getters.
- **Performance**: 2,000–4,000 hours damp-heat stability; flexible and lightweight.
- **Advances (2026)**: Self-healing polymers and nano-additives reduce delamination.

### 3. Atomic Layer Deposition (ALD) Ultra-Thin Barriers
- **Description**: Nanometer-scale Al₂O₃, TiO₂, or hybrid inorganic-organic coatings applied via ALD.
- **Performance**: Exceptional barrier properties (water vapor transmission rate <10⁻⁶ g/m²/day); enables flexible tandems.
- **Leaders**: Swift Solar, Hanwha Qcells — key for next-gen lightweight modules.

### 4. Hybrid & Edge-Sealing Approaches
- **Description**: Combination of ALD + polymer films + advanced edge sealants (e.g., PIB-based or laser-welded glass).
- **Performance**: Best-of-both-worlds — rigid protection + flexibility; <1% degradation after 1,000 hours light soaking + thermal cycling.

### 5. Emerging Self-Healing & Smart Encapsulation
- **Description**: Polymers that autonomously repair micro-cracks; photo-responsive or ion-trapping layers.
- **Progress (2026)**: Lab demos show 50–70% recovery of performance after damage.

## Commercial Readiness (2026)
- Oxford PV and LONGi tandems already use glass-glass + advanced edge sealing.
- Flexible perovskite modules (Swift Solar, Utmo Light) rely heavily on ALD + polymer hybrids.
- Goal: 20–25 year field lifetime with <0.5% annual degradation.
