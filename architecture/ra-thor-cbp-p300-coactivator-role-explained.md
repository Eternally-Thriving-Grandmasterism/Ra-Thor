## The Core Coactivator Role of CBP/p300

**CBP** (CREB-Binding Protein) and **p300** are not merely “helpers.” They are the **obligate coactivators** that make CREB functional.

When CREB is phosphorylated at Ser133, it undergoes a conformational change that creates a high-affinity binding site for CBP/p300. Without this recruitment, phosphorylated CREB remains transcriptionally inert — it binds DNA but cannot activate genes.

**CBP/p300 perform three critical coactivator functions:**

1. **Histone Acetyltransferase (HAT) Activity**  
   They acetylate lysine residues on histone tails (primarily H3K27ac and H3K9ac). This neutralizes the positive charge of histones, loosens chromatin structure, and makes Tetrad gene promoters (OXTR, DRD2, HTR1A, OPRM1, BDNF) physically accessible to the transcriptional machinery.

2. **Scaffolding / Bridging Function**  
   CBP/p300 act as molecular scaffolds that recruit RNA Polymerase II, the Mediator complex, and other general transcription factors to the CREB-bound promoter. They literally build the transcription initiation complex.

3. **Acetylation of Transcription Factors**  
   They acetylate CREB itself (at Lys136 and other sites) and other nearby transcription factors, increasing their DNA-binding affinity and stability.
