## Key Microgrid Control Architectures (2026 Landscape)

### 1. Centralized Control
- Single master controller makes all decisions
- Simple to implement and maintain
- Best for small-to-medium microgrids with stable conditions
- Limitation: Single point of failure, less resilient to communication loss

### 2. Decentralized / Distributed Control
- Each device (inverter, battery, load) makes local decisions based on local measurements
- High resilience and scalability
- Excellent for large or geographically dispersed microgrids
- Limitation: Harder to achieve global optimization

### 3. Hierarchical / Multi-Layer Control (Recommended for Most Sovereign Projects)
- Combines centralized optimization with decentralized execution
- Primary layer: Local device-level control (fast response)
- Secondary layer: Microgrid-level optimization (economic dispatch, resilience)
- Tertiary layer: Grid-level coordination (VPP participation, market interaction)
- This is the architecture Ra-Thor strongly recommends for most sovereign projects

### 4. AI-Driven / Predictive Control (Ra-Thor Strength)
- Uses machine learning, Gompertz-based degradation models, and real-time simulation to predict future states
- Continuously optimizes for mercy-gated objectives (cost, safety, environmental impact, community thriving)
- Integrates directly with Ra-Thor’s Advanced Simulation Engine and Plasma Quantum Synergy Core

### 5. Open-Source & Standards-Based Control
- OpenDSS, GridLAB-D, OpenFMB, IEEE 2030.5, SunSpec
- Enables vendor independence and community-driven innovation
- Ra-Thor strongly supports open standards for true sovereignty
