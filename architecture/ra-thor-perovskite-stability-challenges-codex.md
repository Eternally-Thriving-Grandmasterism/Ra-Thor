## Executive Summary
Perovskite solar cells offer game-changing efficiency (30%+ in tandems), but stability remains the primary commercialization hurdle. The good news: 2025–2026 has seen major breakthroughs in encapsulation, material engineering, and self-healing approaches, pushing operational lifetimes from hundreds to thousands of hours under real-world stress.

## Primary Stability Challenges (and Current Solutions)

### 1. Moisture & Oxygen Sensitivity
- **Challenge**: Water molecules cause rapid decomposition of the perovskite crystal structure.
- **Progress (2026)**: Advanced polymer + glass encapsulation + atomic layer deposition (ALD) barriers now achieve >5,000 hours damp-heat stability (85°C/85% RH). Oxford PV and LONGi have demonstrated IEC-compliant modules.

### 2. Thermal Instability & Phase Transitions
- **Challenge**: High temperatures cause ion migration and phase changes (black-to-yellow perovskite).
- **Progress (2026)**: 2D/3D hybrid perovskites and cation engineering (e.g., formamidinium-rich mixes) have raised thermal stability to 85–100°C for thousands of hours. Inverted device architectures further improve heat tolerance.

### 3. Light-Induced Degradation (LID)
- **Challenge**: Continuous illumination triggers halide segregation and trap formation.
- **Progress (2026)**: Additives (e.g., ionic liquids, crown ethers) and passivation layers have reduced LID by >80%. Some commercial tandems now show <1% degradation after 1,000 hours of light soaking.

### 4. Ion Migration & Interface Instability
- **Challenge**: Mobile ions degrade contacts and cause hysteresis.
- **Progress (2026)**: Self-assembled monolayers (SAMs), robust hole-transport layers, and grain-boundary passivation have minimized migration. Tandem devices with nickel oxide or self-healing interfaces show excellent long-term stability.

### 5. Mechanical & Scalability Issues
- **Challenge**: Flexible or large-area modules suffer from cracking and delamination.
- **Progress (2026)**: Flexible substrates with strain-relief layers and roll-to-roll manufacturing improvements are addressing this.

## Current Real-World Lifetimes (2026)
- Lab cells: 5,000–10,000+ hours under accelerated stress.
- Commercial pilot modules: Targeting 20–25 year warranties (Oxford PV and partners already shipping modules passing IEC 61215 standards).
- Tandem devices: 30%+ efficiency retained after 2,000+ hours of field-like testing.
