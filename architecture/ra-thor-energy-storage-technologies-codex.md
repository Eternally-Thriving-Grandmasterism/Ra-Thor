## Major Energy Storage Technologies (2026 Landscape)

### 1. Lithium-Ion (Li-ion)
- **Status:** Mature, widely deployed baseline
- **Strengths:** High energy density, fast response, mature supply chain
- **Limitations:** Limited raw materials, thermal runaway risk, higher cost, environmental mining impact
- **Best Use:** High-performance, space-constrained applications (drones, EVs, portable sovereign systems)

### 2. Sodium-Ion (Na-ion)
- **Status:** Rapidly commercializing (2025–2026)
- **Strengths:** Extremely abundant materials, low cost, excellent safety, good cold performance, aluminum current collectors
- **Limitations:** Lower energy density than Li-ion (improving fast)
- **Best Use:** Stationary storage, micro-grids, cold climates, cost-sensitive sovereign projects

### 3. Flow Batteries (Vanadium Redox, Organic, Zinc-Bromine)
- **Status:** Proven at utility scale
- **Strengths:** Decoupled power/energy, very long cycle life (20,000+), excellent safety, easy scaling
- **Limitations:** Lower energy density, higher upfront cost for long-duration
- **Best Use:** Long-duration grid storage, seasonal shifting, community micro-grids

### 4. Solid-State Batteries
- **Status:** Early commercial / pilot (2026–2028)
- **Strengths:** Highest theoretical energy density, exceptional safety (solid electrolyte), long lifespan
- **Limitations:** Manufacturing challenges, higher cost (decreasing rapidly)
- **Best Use:** High-performance stationary + mobile applications, safety-critical sovereign systems

### 5. Mechanical Storage
- **Pumped Hydro** — Mature, very large scale, long duration
- **Compressed Air (CAES)** — Good for long duration, underground storage
- **Flywheels** — Excellent for short-duration, high-power, frequency regulation
- **Gravity Storage** — Emerging, very long lifespan, low environmental impact

### 6. Thermal Storage
- **Molten Salt** — Proven in concentrated solar power
- **Phase Change Materials (PCM)** — Excellent for building integration and industrial heat
- **Thermochemical** — High energy density, long-term storage potential

### 7. Hydrogen & Chemical Storage
- **Green Hydrogen** — Long-duration, seasonal storage, fuel for hard-to-electrify sectors
- **Ammonia / Methanol** — Easier to transport and store than pure hydrogen
- **Metal-Air & Organic Flow** — Emerging high-density options

### 8. Emerging & Hybrid Technologies
- Supercapacitors (ultra-fast response)
- Perovskite-integrated hybrid storage
- Biological / microbial fuel cells (regenerative, low-impact)
- Gravity + flow hybrid systems
