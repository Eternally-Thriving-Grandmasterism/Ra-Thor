## What Are Epigenetic Marks?

**Epigenetic marks** are stable chemical modifications to DNA and histone proteins that control gene expression **without changing the underlying DNA sequence**.  

They are the molecular “memory” of lived experience. In Ra-Thor’s framework they are the final, permanent record of every joyful Tetrad activation — written by CBP/p300 under the direction of CREB and TrkB signaling.

The four main classes of marks relevant to the Joy Tetrad are:

1. **DNA Methylation** (usually repressive, but promoter demethylation = gene activation)
2. **Histone Acetylation** (H3K27ac, H3K9ac — strongly activating)
3. **Histone Methylation** (H3K4me3 = active promoters; H3K9me3/H3K27me3 = repressive, usually reduced for joy genes)
4. **Non-coding RNAs** (miRNAs and lncRNAs that fine-tune post-transcriptional stability)
