## Executive Summary
Perovskite solar cells achieve record efficiencies (>34% in tandems) but remain vulnerable to multiple degradation pathways under real-world conditions. The main mechanisms are moisture/oxygen ingress, thermal stress, light-induced degradation, ion migration, interface reactions, and mechanical stress. 2025–2026 advances in material engineering, passivation, and encapsulation have dramatically improved lifetimes, but understanding these root causes is critical for sovereign, long-term Ra-Thor energy systems.

## Primary Degradation Mechanisms (2026 Understanding)

### 1. Moisture & Oxygen Ingress (Hydrolysis)
- Water molecules intercalate into the ABX₃ lattice, breaking hydrogen bonds and decomposing the perovskite into PbI₂, organic salts, and hydrates.
- Rapid under ambient humidity; accelerated by oxygen forming superoxide radicals.
- Most severe extrinsic factor; primary reason early devices failed in minutes.

### 2. Thermal Degradation & Phase Transitions
- Above ~85°C, organic cations (MA/FA) volatilize, leading to PbI₂ formation and irreversible lattice collapse.
- Phase transitions (black → yellow δ-phase in CsPbI₃ or mixed-halide segregation).
- Ion migration is thermally activated, creating defects and recombination centers.

### 3. Light-Induced Degradation (LID) & Halide Segregation
- Illumination triggers halide (I/Br) segregation in mixed-halide perovskites, forming low-bandgap I-rich domains that act as recombination traps.
- Photo-oxidation and trap-state formation reduce Voc and fill factor.
- Especially problematic in wide-bandgap top cells for tandems.

### 4. Ion Migration & Defect Formation
- Mobile ions (I⁻, MA⁺, FA⁺) migrate under electric field, light, or heat, causing hysteresis, phase segregation, and interface corrosion.
- Leads to accumulation at contacts, metallic Pb formation, and non-radiative recombination.

### 5. Interface & Charge Transport Layer Degradation
- Chemical reactions between perovskite and HTL/ETL (e.g., SnO₂ protonation, Au migration, redox reactions).
- Delamination, void formation, and increased series resistance.

### 6. Mechanical Stress & Scalability Issues
- Film cracking, delamination under thermal cycling or flexing.
- Grain boundary weakness and residual strain from crystallization.

## 2026 Mitigation Progress
- Advanced encapsulation (glass-glass, ALD barriers, self-healing polymers) → >5,000 hours damp-heat stability.
- 2D/3D heterostructures, additives (ionic liquids, crown ethers), and passivation layers suppress ion migration and halide segregation.
- Inverted architectures and robust SAMs improve interface stability.
- Commercial tandems (Oxford PV, LONGi) now approach 20–25 year warranties.
