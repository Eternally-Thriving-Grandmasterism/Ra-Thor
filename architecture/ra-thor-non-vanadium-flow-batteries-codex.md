## Executive Summary
While Vanadium Redox Flow Batteries (VRFBs) currently dominate the long-duration flow battery market, a diverse range of **non-vanadium chemistries** are rapidly advancing in 2026. These alternatives offer lower material costs, reduced supply-chain risks, and sometimes higher energy density — making them highly relevant for sovereign energy systems that prioritize abundance, affordability, and environmental harmony.

## Key Non-Vanadium Flow Battery Chemistries (2026 Landscape)

### 1. Organic Flow Batteries (Quinone, Viologen, TEMPO-based)
- **Key Chemistries:** Anthraquinone-2,7-disulfonate (AQDS), TEMPO, viologen derivatives
- **Key Strengths:** Low material cost, abundant organic molecules, tunable redox potentials, no heavy metals
- **Key Limitations:** Lower energy density than vanadium, stability challenges in some formulations
- **Best Use:** Cost-sensitive stationary storage, community microgrids, future mass deployment

### 2. Zinc-Bromine Flow Battery
- **Chemistry:** Zn²⁺/Zn (negative) + Br₂/Br⁻ (positive)
- **Key Strengths:** Higher energy density than all-vanadium, lower cost, proven at scale
- **Key Limitations:** Bromine crossover and toxicity management required, slightly lower cycle life than VRFB
- **Best Use:** Medium-duration (4–12 hours) where space or cost is a constraint

### 3. Iron-Chromium (Fe-Cr) Flow Battery
- **Chemistry:** Fe²⁺/Fe³⁺ (positive) + Cr²⁺/Cr³⁺ (negative)
- **Key Strengths:** Very low material cost (iron + chromium abundant), good safety profile
- **Key Limitations:** Lower voltage and energy density, hydrogen evolution side reactions
- **Best Use:** Very large-scale, cost-critical long-duration storage

### 4. All-Iron Flow Battery
- **Chemistry:** Fe²⁺/Fe³⁺ on both sides (same electrolyte)
- **Key Strengths:** Extremely low cost, no crossover issues (same species), fully recyclable
- **Key Limitations:** Lower voltage, early commercial stage (2026)
- **Best Use:** Ultra-low-cost, large-scale stationary storage (emerging leader)

### 5. Emerging Hybrids (Zinc-Iron, Organic-Metal, etc.)
- **Key Strengths:** Combining best traits of multiple chemistries, rapid innovation
- **Key Limitations:** Still mostly in pilot/R&D phase
- **Best Use:** Future sovereign systems seeking optimal cost/performance balance
