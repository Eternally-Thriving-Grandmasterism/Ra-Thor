## Executive Summary
Tandem (multi-junction) solar cells stack two or more absorbers with different bandgaps to capture a broader portion of the solar spectrum, breaking the single-junction Shockley-Queisser limit (~33%). Perovskite-based tandems are the fastest path to 30%+ commercial efficiency, directly enabling the exponential clean-energy abundance vision.

## Main Tandem Architectures (2026 Status)

### 1. Perovskite-on-Silicon (Most Advanced & Commercial)
- **Structure**: Wide-bandgap perovskite top cell (1.6–1.7 eV) on narrow-bandgap silicon bottom cell (1.1 eV).
- **Record Efficiency**: 34.85% (LONGi, NREL-certified, April 2025).
- **Commercial Modules**: 24–27% (Oxford PV, LONGi, Hanwha Qcells pilots in 2026).
- **Advantages**: Leverages existing silicon manufacturing; highest near-term impact.
- **Challenges**: Current matching, top-cell stability, interface recombination.

### 2. All-Perovskite Tandems (Flexible & Low-Cost Potential)
- **Structure**: Two perovskite layers with different bandgaps (e.g., 1.55 eV + 1.25 eV).
- **Record Efficiency**: >30% (multiple labs in 2025–2026).
- **Commercial Outlook**: Early pilots 2026–2027; strong for lightweight/flexible applications.
- **Advantages**: Lower material costs, roll-to-roll manufacturing possible.
- **Challenges**: Stability of narrow-bandgap bottom cell, toxicity concerns (lead).

### 3. Triple-Junction & Multi-Junction Tandems
- **Structure**: Perovskite-perovskite-silicon or perovskite-CIGS stacks.
- **Record Efficiency**: Approaching 35–38% in lab (2025–2026).
- **Status**: Mostly research; early commercial interest for space/high-efficiency niches.
- **Advantages**: Highest theoretical efficiency potential.
- **Challenges**: Complex current matching, manufacturing yield, cost.

### 4. Other Emerging Tandems
- Perovskite + CIGS, perovskite + organic, perovskite + quantum-dot.
- Focus areas: Flexible, lightweight, building-integrated PV (BIPV).

## Key Players & 2026 Commercial Roadmap

- **LONGi**: World-record holder; scaling GW-scale tandem lines.
- **Hanwha Qcells, Trina Solar, Swift Solar**: Aggressive pilot-to-mass-production plans for 2026–2027.
