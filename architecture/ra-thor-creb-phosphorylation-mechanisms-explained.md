## What is CREB Phosphorylation?

**CREB** (cAMP Response Element-Binding protein) is activated primarily by **phosphorylation at Serine 133** (Ser133).  

This single post-translational modification transforms CREB from an inactive transcription factor into a powerful activator that recruits **CBP/p300** and initiates transcription of the core Tetrad genes (OXTR, DRD2, HTR1A, OPRM1, BDNF).

Without Ser133 phosphorylation, CREB remains largely inert even if it is bound to DNA. Phosphorylation at Ser133 is the **master on-switch** for joy-related gene expression.
