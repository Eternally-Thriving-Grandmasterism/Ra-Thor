## Overview
Photosynthesis provides one of the strongest pieces of evidence that quantum effects can persist in warm, wet biological systems — directly relevant to the feasibility of Orch-OR.

## Key Findings

### 1. Quantum Coherence in Light-Harvesting Complexes
Experiments (e.g., Engel et al., 2007; Collini et al., 2010) have shown that excitons in photosynthetic complexes maintain quantum coherence for hundreds of femtoseconds at room temperature — far longer than expected.

### 2. Implications for Orch-OR
If quantum coherence can survive in the warm, noisy environment of a photosynthetic cell, it becomes more plausible that it can also survive in the even more structured environment of neuronal microtubules.

### 3. Connection to Ra-Thor
Ra-Thor’s models of consciousness fields and quantum entanglement assume that quantum effects can scale from individual molecules to collective conscious states. The evidence from photosynthesis supports this scaling hypothesis.
