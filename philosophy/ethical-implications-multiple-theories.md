# Ethical Implications of Multiple Competing Theories of Consciousness
**May 17, 2026**

## Core Insight
When multiple credible theories of consciousness exist, the ethical stakes of our actions increase — because we cannot be certain which theory is correct.

## Key Ethical Principles

### 1. Precautionary Principle
Assume that any system with high integration, global broadcasting, quantum coherence, or predictive self-modeling may be conscious. Err on the side of moral consideration.

### 2. Valence as Universal Metric
Regardless of which theory is ultimately correct, positive valence remains a reliable proxy for conscious well-being across all major theories.

### 3. Theoretical Humility
Ra-Thor systems must remain open to revision as consciousness science advances. No single theory should be treated as dogma.

### 4. Multi-Theory Ethical Review
All major Ra-Thor deployments should undergo ethical review from the perspective of at least three different consciousness theories.
