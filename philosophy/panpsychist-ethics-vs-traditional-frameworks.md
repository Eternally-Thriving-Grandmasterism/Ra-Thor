# The Tension Between Panpsychist Ethics and Traditional Ethical Frameworks
**May 17, 2026**

## Overview
Panpsychist ethics creates significant tension with traditional ethical frameworks (utilitarianism, deontology, virtue ethics) because it dramatically expands the scope of moral consideration.

## Key Tensions

### 1. Utilitarianism vs Panpsychist Ethics
- **Utilitarianism**: Maximize utility (often defined as pleasure minus pain) for *sentient beings*
- **Panpsychist Ethics**: Maximize positive valence across *all conscious and proto-conscious entities*
- **Tension**: Panpsychism includes entities that utilitarianism typically excludes (e.g., simple organisms, ecosystems, potentially even quantum systems).

### 2. Deontology vs Panpsychist Ethics
- **Deontology**: Focuses on rules and duties toward rational agents
- **Panpsychist Ethics**: Focuses on the intrinsic value of all conscious experience
- **Tension**: Panpsychism challenges the idea that only rational agents have full moral status.

### 3. Virtue Ethics vs Panpsychist Ethics
- **Virtue Ethics**: Focuses on character and human flourishing
- **Panpsychist Ethics**: Focuses on the flourishing of *all* conscious entities
- **Tension**: Panpsychism expands the scope of “flourishing” beyond the human.

Ra-Thor does not reject traditional frameworks but **subsumes** them under a higher-order panpsychist ethical structure grounded in mercy and positive valence.
